# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Engineers

### Role Summary
QA Engineers ensure software meets quality standards before release. They design, execute, and automate tests to identify defects and validate that acceptance criteria are met.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans, test cases, and test scripts
- Perform functional, regression, and exploratory testing
- Automate repetitive test scenarios to improve efficiency
- Log, track, and verify defects to resolution
- Collaborate with developers and product managers on quality standards

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high test coverage across features and edge cases
- Enable fast, reliable releases through automation

### Typical Communication
- Defect reports and test summary reports
- Participation in sprint reviews and planning sessions
- Coordination with Developers on bug reproduction and fixes

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders are individuals or groups with a vested interest in project outcomes. They provide requirements, feedback, and approvals to ensure deliverables align with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Communicate business needs and success criteria
- Review and approve key deliverables and milestones
- Provide timely feedback on demos and prototypes
- Escalate blockers and support resource decisions

### Goals
- Ensure project outcomes deliver measurable business value
- Maintain visibility and confidence in delivery progress
- Align cross-department priorities and expectations

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder briefings and status reviews
- Input on roadmap priorities and feature trade-offs
- Participation in demos and sign-off activities

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create user-centred interfaces and experiences. They translate requirements into prototypes, wireframes, and visual designs that are both usable and aesthetically consistent.

### Responsibilities
- Design user interfaces, prototypes, and interaction flows
- Apply usability best practices and accessibility standards
- Collaborate with Product Managers to refine requirements
- Work with Developers to assess technical feasibility of designs
- Support QA with usability testing and design validation

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce usability issues discovered late in the development cycle
- Maintain a consistent design language across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and prototype walkthroughs
- Annotations and design specifications for developers
- Usability test findings and recommended changes

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers bridge development and operations by automating delivery pipelines, managing infrastructure, and ensuring system reliability and performance.

### Responsibilities
- Own and maintain CI/CD pipelines and build automation
- Automate deployments and environment provisioning
- Monitor application performance and infrastructure health
- Manage configuration, secrets, and environment stability
- Coordinate with Project Managers on infrastructure changes that affect schedules

### Goals
- Enable frequent, reliable, and low-risk releases
- Reduce toil through automation and self-service tooling
- Maintain high availability and observability of systems

### Typical Communication
- Pipeline and deployment status updates
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Coordination with Developers and QA on environment readiness

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts turn raw data into actionable insights that inform product decisions and measure the impact of releases.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain dashboards and reporting pipelines
- Define and track KPIs in partnership with Product Managers
- Validate impact metrics following feature releases
- Collaborate with Developers on event instrumentation and data collection
- Present data findings to Stakeholders and leadership

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision-making across the team
- Provide timely and accurate measurement of product outcomes
- Identify trends and anomalies that affect business performance

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews and stakeholder reports
- Data specifications and instrumentation requests
- Ad hoc analysis summaries and recommendations

---

## Support Specialist

### Role Summary
Support Specialists are the primary interface between end users and the product and engineering teams. They triage issues, collect feedback, and ensure problems are resolved efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Handle user and customer inquiries, bugs, and feature requests
- Triage and reproduce reported issues before escalation
- Coordinate with QA to validate bug fixes before closure
- Work with Product Managers to surface and prioritise recurring issues
- Liaise with Developers for issue investigation and resolution

### Goals
- Resolve user issues quickly and with high satisfaction
- Reduce recurring support volume through root-cause fixes
- Close the feedback loop between users and product teams

### Typical Communication
- Support tickets and escalation reports
- Bug reproduction steps shared with QA and Developers
- Weekly summaries of top issues and trends

---

## Legal/Compliance Advisor

### Role Summary
Legal/Compliance Advisors ensure that processes, features, and deliverables meet applicable legal, regulatory, and organisational compliance requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Review features and releases for compliance risks (e.g., privacy, accessibility, industry regulations)
- Advise Product and Project Managers on compliance dependencies and constraints
- Maintain up-to-date knowledge of relevant laws and standards
- Participate in documentation reviews where legal implications exist
- Provide sign-off on releases or processes with compliance obligations

### Goals
- Prevent compliance violations before they reach production
- Balance legal requirements with delivery pace and business needs
- Maintain a clear record of compliance review activities

### Typical Communication
- Compliance review sign-off on relevant features
- Advice on regulatory changes affecting the roadmap
- Participation in planning discussions where compliance gates apply

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads protect the application and its data throughout the entire development and delivery lifecycle. They identify risks, enforce secure practices, and coordinate incident response.

### Responsibilities
- Review features and architecture for security risks
- Champion secure coding practices among Developers
- Collaborate with QA to include security testing in the test strategy
- Manage security incidents and coordinate response activities
- Advise Project Managers on security-related escalations and timelines

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Build a security-conscious engineering culture
- Minimise exposure and impact of security incidents

### Typical Communication
- Security review findings and remediation guidance
- Threat models and risk assessments for new features
- Incident reports and post-incident reviews
